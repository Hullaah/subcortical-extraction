PHASE 2: CLASSICAL METHODS (Papers 4-6)
------------------------------------

4. Babalola, K. O., Patenaude, B., Aljabar, P., Schnabel, J., Kennedy, D., & Crum, W.
   "Comparison and Evaluation of Segmentation Techniques for Subcortical Structures in Brain MRI"
   MICCAI, 2008.

5. Kim, E. Y., Ye, J. C., Kim, E., & Park, K. H.
   "Semiautomatic Segmentation of Brain Subcortical Structures from High-Field MRI"

6. Chupin, M., Gérardin, E., Cuingnet, R., Tuppin, P., Cointepas, Y., Lehéricy, S., ... & Colliot, O.

   and Mild Cognitive Impairment Applied on Data from ADNI"
   Hippocampus, 19(6), 579-587, 2009.

------------------------------------
PHASE 3: TRANSITION TO DEEP LEARNING (Papers 7-9)
------------------------------------

7. Litjens, G., Kooi, T., Bejnordi, B. E., Setio, A. A. A., Ciompi, F., Ghafoorian, M., ... & Sánchez, C. I.
   "A Survey on Deep Learning in Medical Image Analysis"
   Medical Image Analysis, 42, 60-88, 2017.
   NOTE: Read this before touching any deep learning paper. It is the map.

8. Ronneberger, O., Fischer, P., & Brox, T.
   "U-Net: Convolutional Networks for Biomedical Image Segmentation"
   MICCAI, 2015.
   NOTE: This is the foundational paper. Everything after builds on this.

9. Çiçek, Ö., Abdulkadir, A., Lienkamp, S. S., Brox, T., & Ronneberger, O.
   "3D U-Net: Learning Dense Volumetric Segmentation from Sparse Annotation"
   MICCAI, 2016.
   NOTE: Direct extension of U-Net to 3D volumes — exactly your use case.

19. Dice, L. R.
    "Measures of the Amount of Ecologic Association Between Species"

    present your results. Critical for making the project research grade.
